ⓘ Method, sources & assumptions

Method — three layers, kept separate

Measured figures come directly from a published dataset and are cited. Modelled figures are derived by inference or spatial join; each carries its assumption chain and a confidence label. Judgement inputs are planner assertions, marked as such — none are applied yet. No blended score exists: a route gets target boardings (volume) and an audience index (concentration), never one number.

How the audience builder scores

Each subzone's share of your selected audience (age bands × sex, housing groups) is indexed against the national share (100 = national average). Context layers (planning-area geometry) multiply in by geometric mean. Stops inherit their subzone's index; a route's target boardings = Σ allocated stop boardings × index. Combined levers are treated as independent, which overstates concentration where they correlate — multi-lever audiences are directional.

Sources

    Stated assumptions

      Confidence labels

      Subzone levers start at medium; adding a planning-area context layer moves to low. The label widens when less than 80% of a route's allocated volume has audience data, or when more than 10% comes from index-capped subzones. Nothing is labelled better than medium: the residence join (boarders ≈ catchment residents) is unverified and applies to every figure. With no levers selected the ranking is pure measured volume ("everyone").

      Consumer behaviour levers

      Each behaviour lever is a stop-catchment density index: the count of relevant places (supermarkets, hawker centres, pre-schools, pharmacies…) within 400 m of a stop, indexed to the volume-weighted network average. Locations come from cited registries and are observed; the behaviour is inferred — being near a supermarket is evidence of grocery-shopper presence, not proof of shopping. These levers score at stop level (finer than subzone), always carry at most low confidence, and use an additive-smoothed density ratio (count+1 over baseline+1), floored at 25 and capped at 300, so sparse layers grade smoothly instead of flipping between floor and cap. A spend-propensity join from the Household Expenditure Survey 2023 (by dwelling type) has been identified but its by-dwelling tables are not yet machine-readable on data.gov.sg; it is the named next data source, not silently proxied.

      Ethnicity

      Ethnic composition is shown per route and subzone as creative and language guidance only — festive timing, language mix. It is not a selectable audience filter and enters no score, by design.

      Resonance

      Not scored — it cannot be derived from open data. It is measured post-campaign (brand-lift vs matched control), never asserted pre-campaign.
